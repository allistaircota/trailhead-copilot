# Trailhead Supply Co. — Gear Care Guide

_Last updated: January 2026_

Good gear lasts for years when you look after it. Here's how to keep your Trailhead
equipment trail-ready. Following this guidance also helps preserve your warranty
coverage, since damage from improper care is not covered by the Trailhead Guarantee.

## Tents

- **Dry before storage.** Always let your tent dry completely before packing it away.
  Storing a damp tent leads to mildew and coating breakdown.
- **Clean gently.** Use a sponge with cool water and mild, non-detergent soap. Never
  machine wash a tent.
- **Store loosely.** Keep the tent in a breathable bag in a cool, dry place — not
  compressed for long periods.

## Sleeping Bags

- **Store uncompressed.** Long-term compression damages loft. Store in the large mesh or
  cotton sack, not the stuff sack.
- **Wash rarely and carefully.** Use a front-loading machine on gentle with a
  down-specific or tech wash. Dry on low with clean tennis balls to restore loft (down
  bags).

## Footwear

- **Clean after muddy trips.** Remove dirt with a soft brush and water. Caked mud dries
  out leather and breaks down membranes.
- **Dry naturally.** Air dry away from direct heat. Never put boots next to a fire or
  heater — high heat cracks adhesives and leather.
- **Re-treat leather** periodically with a suitable conditioner or waterproofing wax.

## Down & Insulated Apparel

- **Re-loft after washing.** Tumble dry low with dryer balls so the down redistributes.
- **Store loosely** on a hanger or in a breathable bag.

## Restoring Water Resistance (DWR)

Over time, the Durable Water Repellent (DWR) finish on jackets and tents wears off and
fabric "wets out." To restore it:

1. Clean the item first (dirt blocks DWR performance).
2. Apply a spray-on or wash-in DWR treatment.
3. Activate with low heat (tumble dry low or a warm iron with a cloth barrier), per the
   product's care label.

## Stoves & Cookware

- **Cool fully before packing.**
- **Hand wash** cookware; avoid abrasive pads on non-stick surfaces.
- **Check fuel connections** for wear before each trip and store fuel canisters
  separately.
